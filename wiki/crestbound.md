# 🛡️ Crestbound

**Crestbound** is the collecting game built into the Server Assistant portal. The
**XP** you earn just by being active in Discord becomes a currency you can spend —
open **booster packs**, collect ornate **Crests**, and chase a complete Chapter. It's
a reward for taking part, with nothing extra to buy.

> **Powered by your XP.** Crestbound runs on the same account-wide XP you earn from
> chatting and from [voting for Server Assistant]({{ '/wiki/leveling/' | relative_url }}#earning-xp).
> Opening a pack spends that XP; there's no separate purchase and no Premium
> required — if you've earned XP, you can play.

---

## The world of Crestbound {#lore}

> Long before Aldermarch knew quiet, its countless hearth-halls were beset by
> trolls — small, pink-furred, restless things that lived only to unsettle the
> gathered and tear down whatever others built. Against them rose the Order:
> armored wardens who rode hall to hall holding the peace, until the troubles
> swelled into the **First War**. No blade could win it outright — so in one last
> working the Order bound both the trolls' mischief and the might of the knights
> who answered it into heraldic **Crests**, sealing each champion and troublemaker
> behind the sigil it had once fought under.
>
> For generations the Crests lay quiet, their embers banked but never cold. Now
> they are warming again — and somewhere a hand moves among them, gathering Crest
> after Crest toward an end not yet written.

That hand is yours. The first Chapter, **Chapter I: Embers of the First War**, holds
a sweeping cast of **Crests** to track down — each Chapter brings roughly **150–200**
of them across the four rarities. Future Chapters carry the story on.

> **Coming later — Wardecks.** Collecting is just the start. A future **battle mode**
> is on the way, where you'll assemble a **Wardeck** — a 50-Crest battle group drawn
> from your collection — and put it to the test. That part isn't here yet; for now,
> Crestbound is all about building your collection.

## Where to find it {#where}

Crestbound lives in the **customer portal**. Sign in with Discord, and you'll find
**Crestbound** under the **🎮 XP Games** section of the portal — open it to jump
straight into your collection.

## Your collection {#collection}

The **My Collection** view shows the whole Chapter at once. Crests you own appear in
full, framed by a colour that marks their rarity; Crests you haven't found yet sit in
their place as a grey **“?”**, so you can always see exactly what's still out there.
A counter at the top tracks your progress and shows your spendable XP.

### Looking at a Crest {#crest-detail}

Tap any Crest you own to see it up close — its name, its rarity, its artwork, a line
of ability text and a touch of flavour. **Creature** Crests carry an attack and
health value; **Spell** Crests don't fight, so they show their type instead of
stats. Rare and mythic Crests catch the light with a subtle sheen.

## Booster packs {#boosters}

You build your collection by opening **booster packs**. Each pack costs **200 XP** and
contains **5 Crests** — and the last Crest in every pack is **always rare or better**,
so there's a guaranteed highlight in each one.

The four rarities, from most common to most prized:

  <tr><td><strong>Uncommon</strong></td><td><span style="color:#3fb568;">green</span></td><td>A step up in scarcity.</td></tr>

**Opening a pack.** Press the booster button in your collection. The button only lights
up when you have enough XP — if you're short, it tells you so (you'll need 200 XP).
You'll be asked to confirm the spend, the pack opens with a little reveal, and the five
Crests are added to your collection.

## Burning spare Crests {#burning}

Got duplicates piling up? **Burn** a spare Crest to recover some XP. Open a Crest you
own and tap **🔥 Burn for N XP** — the copy leaves your collection and the XP lands
back in your balance, ready to spend on more packs.

The XP you get back depends on the rarity:

<table class="cmd-args" style="max-width:420px;">
  <tr><th>Rarity</th><th>XP recovered</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Common</td><td>5 XP</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Uncommon</td><td>12 XP</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Rare</td><td>30 XP</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Mythic</td><td>75 XP</td></tr>
</table>

<p style="color:var(--fg-1);font-size:.9rem;line-height:1.55;"><strong>Burning is for spares, not for farming.</strong> The XP you get back is well below a pack's price, so it's a way to tidy up duplicates — not a shortcut to free packs. There's a confirmation step every time, and if you're about to burn your <em>only</em> copy of a Crest the game warns you first, so you won't lose one by accident.</p>
